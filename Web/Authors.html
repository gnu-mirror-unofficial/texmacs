The GNU TeXmacs system, which is part of the GNU project, was designed and written by Joris van der Hoeven. The system was inspired both by the TeX system, written by D. Knuth, and by Emacs, written by R. Stallman. Special thanks goes to them, as well as to the C.N.R.S. (the French national institute for scientific research), which employs me and authorized me to distribute this program under the GNU General Public License.

Further thanks go to the following contributors:

  • Andrey Grozin for his constant help on many issues: interfaces to several computer algebra systems, support for the russian language, tools for the manipulation of dictionaries, etc.
  • Jean-Claude Fernandez, Fabien Salvi and the other persons from the CRI for hosting and administrating TeXmacs.
  • Dan Grayson, with whose help I implemented communications with computer algebra systems via pipes, who provided some money support for TeXmacs, and who made many useful comments and suggestions.
  • Karim Belabas, with whom I designed and developed the first protocol for interfacing TeXmacs with scientific computation or computer algebra systems. He also implemented the interface with the pari system.
  • Ralf Treinen for supporting the debian package for TeXmacs and for his corrections in the german dictionary.
  • Christophe Merlet and Bo Forslund for their help with making a portable TeXmacs.spec.
  • Dan Ignat for translating the menus and some of the help-files into Rumanian.

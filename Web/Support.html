User support


In order to get user support on TeXmacs, the best thing you can do (especially when you are a novice) is to suscribe to the TeXmacs mailing list texmacs-users@texmacs.org, by sending an email to sympa@texmacs.org with a subject of the form 

    SUBSCRIBE texmacs-users Your Full Name
You may unsubscribe later on by sending an email to sympa@texmacs.org with 
    SIGNOFF texmacs-users
as subject. The texmacs-users mailing list archives are also available without subscription.

Similarly, if you are just interested in receiving information about major new releases of TeXmacs, or other major relevant information related to the program, you may suscribe to the mailing list info@texmacs.org. This is done by sending an email to sympa@texmacs.org with a subject of the form 

    SUBSCRIBE texmacs-info Your Full Name
The number of messages you will receive in this case should not exceed something like four a year. Again, you may unsuscribe yourself by sending an email to sympa@texmacs.org with 
    SIGNOFF texmacs-info
as subject.

For more information about how to use the mailing lists, please send an email to sympa@texmacs.org with HELP as it's subject. For support on TeXmacs, you may also directly contact the TeXmacs developers.


A new and efficient (though experimental) way to get more documentation or support for TeXmacs, is to consult the TeXmacs Wiki. This is an interactive forum where users can ask questions about specific topics, but also participate themselves to the documentation effort and to answering questions from other users. In the future, the whole TeXmacs website might work in a similar way.
